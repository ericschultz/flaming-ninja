Creating community from Jim Jagielski, Outercurve Director

Additional earlier presentations:
  • Open World Forum 2011: "FOSS Foundations Enable Community Growth" FOSS projects grow until they reach a certain size but can grow no further. FOSS Foundations enable projects to grow to reach their potential by providing the legal structures and tools to enable corporate contribution and wider adoption. This talk looks at a brief history of foundations in the FOSS space, and what tools they provide to help community projects grow. Presentation Slides (PDF), September 2011
  • Transfer Summit 2011: "Symbian: collaboration, open, closed, dead?" Discusses the challenges of moving from a closed proprietary model of software creation to an open source collaborative approach centered in a non-profit foundation for software IP management. Presentation Slides (PDF), September 2011
  • Open Source Junction 2011: "The Context for Open Source and Mobile Business Models" Presents an overview of open source software in the context of mobile business models and the role for non-profit foundations. Presentation Slides (PDF), March 2011
  • Transfer Summit 2010: Presenting the Outercurve Foundation (then called the Codeplex Foundation) and how open source foundations help manage software IP to encourage community growth from commercial participants. Presentation Slides (PDF)
