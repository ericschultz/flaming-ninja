Wakefield, MA. and Las Vegas, NV.  April 12, 2011 - The Outercurve Foundation today announced the release of version 1.1 of the Orchard project. One of five projects in the foundation’s ASP.NET Open Source Gallery, Orchard is a free, open source, community-based project aimed at delivering applications and reusable components on the ASP.NET platform.  

Orchard 1.1, a .NET-based CMS application, allows users to rapidly create content-driven websites . It includes an extensibility framework that allows developers to provide additional functionality through module extensions and themes. Orchard 1.1 includes a number of new features and enhancements, including a fresh design of the administration panel, a rapid way to set up a new Orchard site using “recipes”, additional extensibility hooks for developers, and flexible “shape tracing” tools for designers to inspect and customize the rendering of an Orchard site. Orchard 1.1 was announced during the Microsoft Web Development Conference (MIX) in Las Vegas, and is available from the Orchard Project site at http://orchardproject.net or http://orchard.codeplex.com. 

“Our goals with Orchard 1.1 were to address community feedback, improve design and usability of administration, and simplify setup and reduce the time it takes to configure a new site. We streamlined the workflow to install modules and themes, manage widgets, and create lists of content. We also made improvements targeted at deployment and hosting,” said Bradley Millington, project lead and manager of the ASP.NET Open Source Gallery. “We are excited to make this announcement at MIX11, and also to announce the winners of the Orchard Module Challenge. We are very impressed with the modules and websites participants created, and pleased to see them contributed to the Orchard Gallery for everyone in the community to enjoy.”

The Orchard Module Challenge included competitions for writing and contributing a module to the Orchard project.  First place for the “Advanced Menu” module was awarded to Piotr Szmyd, followed in second place by “EDB Smooth Streaming” by Daniel Parashkevov, with “Image Gallery” by Gabriel Eduardo Chites de Mello winning third place. The Orchard Module challenge was sponsored and operated by Microsoft Corporation. All entries are listed at http://orchardproject.net/omc. 

The Orchard CMS meets the needs of end-users, designers, developers, and Web professionals alike. Orchard is delivered as part of the ASP.NET Open Source Gallery and is licensed under the OSI-approved New BSD license.

“Orchard has an active and growing community working to define applications and extensions to extend and improve the functionality of the Orchard NET-based CMS application,” said Paula Hunter, Executive Director, Outercurve Foundation. “The project team rapidly created a .NET based CMS that makes it easy to develop content-driven websites. The Outercurve Foundation is a perfect place for Orchard contributors to collaborate to improve and enhance the Orchard CMS.”

The Outercurve Foundation has three galleries and 11 projects. Galleries include the ASP.NET Open Source Gallery (five projects), the Systems Infrastructure and Integration Gallery (three projects) and the recently-announced Research Accelerators Gallery (three projects.)  

For more information on the Outercurve Foundation, visit www.outercurve.org.
