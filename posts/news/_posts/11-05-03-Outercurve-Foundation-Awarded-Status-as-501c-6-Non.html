Wakefield, MA.  May 3, 2011 - The Outercurve Foundation today announced it has been granted tax-exempt status under section 501 (c) (6) of the United States Internal Revenue Code. 

Non-profit 501 (c) (6) status, which provides tax exemption for business leagues, chambers of commerce, real estate boards and boards of trade whose ‘activities are directed to the improvement of business conditions of one or more lines of business rather than the performance of particular services for individual persons’, enables the Outercurve Foundation to operate as an independent, not-for-profit organization whose work is conducted for the broad benefit of the open source community. 

“With its tax-exempt status confirmed by the IRS, the Outercurve Foundation officially joins the ranks of the leading open source foundations, established to work independently on behalf of open source developers, companies which develop software, and users of free and open source (FOSS) software,” said Paula Hunter, Executive Director, Outercurve Foundation. “We are pleased the foundation has been recognized as an organization created to increase participation in open source projects, to the benefit of all in the software development community.”
