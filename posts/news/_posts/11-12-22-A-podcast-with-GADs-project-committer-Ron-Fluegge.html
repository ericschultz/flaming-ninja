We talked with Ron Fluegge, committer of the GADS project in the DSLI Gallery....

I wrote The GADS Project as a commercial application just over 25 years ago, and this is our best opportunity to make it as an open source project. We GPLv2 because it’s the license we've seen grant the most freedom for the people who wanted to get access to the source code.

GADS was written to allow small and large power generating companies to provide federally-mandated data to what's called The North American Electric Reliability Council. GADS is a real benefit for those companies as an open source project, because they can all use it at minimal or no cost.

When it came to finding a foundation to support GADS, everyone I contacted suggested Outercurve because it’s an open source foundation that understands .NET projects.
