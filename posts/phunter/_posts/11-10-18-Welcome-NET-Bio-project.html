 I want to take a moment and welcome the .NET Bio project team to the Outercurve Foundation’s Research Accelerators Gallery.

Formerly known as the Microsoft Biology Foundation, this project enables researchers and scientists to share data and development methodologies to support a range of life sciences research projects.

The .NET Bio project is a language-neutral bioinformatics toolkit built as an extension to the Microsoft .NET Framework. .NET Bio includes parsers for common bioinformatics file formats; algorithms for manipulating DNA, RNA, and protein sequences; and connectors to biological web services, such as NCBI BLAST.

In addition to Microsoft Research contributions, there were several academic and commercial contributions included in the ownership transfer to the Outercurve Foundation. This project is overseen by a technical Advisory Board, including representatives from Cornell, University of Queensland, Johnson & Johnson, Illumina, and the initial sponsor - Microsoft Corporation.

.NET Bio is now in a position to expand its reach across academic, science and research communities, and we wish the team continued success. For questions about the project, contact the Project Manager: Rick Benge at: a-rickbe ”at” Microsoft “dot”com.
