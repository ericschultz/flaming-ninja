--- title: "ChronoZoom enrolls in K-12" alias: /Blogs/EntryId/111/ChronoZoom-enrolls-in-K-12 ---

The ChronoZoom project leaders have announced curriculum modules for K-12 educators to make teaching history more creative and interactive. It uses custom timelines and lesson plan templates to immerse students in pivotal world events.

Microsoft Research (MSR), the University of California Berkeley, and Moscow University created ChronoZoom to help uncover the crosscurrents of history that exist among disparate data sets – a capability that makes it easier to study “Big History.” ChronoZoom displays these insights on a timeline that reveals interrelations throughout geography, culture and history, as well as across multiple disciplines of academic research. Now, Big History is small enough for high schools.

The three new K-12 focused ChronoZoom modules, as per the project team, are:

  • "The Causes of World War I, which teaches about causality and multiple perspectives
  • Atlantic Encounters, which presents a more abstract introduction to historical thinking by studying the moments when two cultures meet and how the meeting changes both cultures
  • The ChronoZoomers Guild, which provides lesson templates and associated materials to create an immersive experience involving time travel and the alteration of pivotal events in world history.”
ChronoZoom won the South by Southwest (SXSW) Interactive Award for Educational Resource in March. The award recognizes the years’ best new digital work and celebrates technologists who are building tomorrow's interactive trends. It is an open source project supported by Outercurve Foundation, and history teachers can freely download the curriculum and experiment with ChronoZoom.
