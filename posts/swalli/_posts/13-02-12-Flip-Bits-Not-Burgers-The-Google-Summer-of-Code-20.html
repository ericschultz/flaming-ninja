--- title: "Flip Bits, Not Burgers: The Google Summer of Code 2013 is Coming Soon" alias: /Blogs/EntryId/83/Flip-Bits-Not-Burgers-The-Google-Summer-of-Code-2013-is-Coming-Soon ---

Today Google announced the 2013 edition of the Google Summer of Code. Google created the Summer of Code in 2005.  It is designed to help students in several ways:

  • Inspire student developers to begin working with open source projects.
  • Expose students to real-world development communities and situations.
  • Give programming students the opportunity to do job-related work.

Last year, the Outercurve Foundation was honored to be chosen as a mentoring organization for its first time.  We were awarded two slots and during the student proposal phase Outercurve mentors narrowed the candidates down to:

  • Irina Grosu from the Faculty of Computer Science, Iasi, Romania with her submission to add modules to Outercurve Mayhem to integrate Microsoft Office, the Google API, Bluetooth connectivity, and Skype.   [The GSoC proposal is here.]
  • Cristina Turken from  the Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Transilvania University of Brasov, Romania with her submission to create a GUI-based desktop application that allows software publishers to create a Outercurve CoApp package, (instead of using the command line tools). [The GSoC proposal is here.]

We are going to apply again this year.  I encourage all our Outercurve Foundation projects to consider participating.  We will be starting the discussion with projects over the next couple of weeks.  Likewise, if you're a student, please consider sending in a proposal for an Outercurve project.  If you know a student, please encourage them to get involved in the Google Summer of Code.  There's a great FAQ for the Summer of Code here.   Watch this space over the next couple of weeks for more announcements.
