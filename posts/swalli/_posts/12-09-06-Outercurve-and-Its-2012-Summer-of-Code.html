--- title: "Outercurve and Its 2012 Summer of Code" alias: /Blogs/EntryId/61/Outercurve-and-Its-2012-Summer-of-Code ---

The Google Summer of Code 2012 (GSoC 2012) is coming to an end.  Outercurve was accepted as a mentoring organization for this year’s Google Summer of Code, being awarded two slots for student projects.  Of the project proposals we received, a number of students stood out in debating and evolving their proposals with Outercurve mentors over the submission time.  We accepted:

  • Irina Grosu from the Faculty of Computer Science, Iasi, Romania with her submission to add modules to Outercurve Mayhem to integrate Microsoft Office, the Google API, Bluetooth connectivity, and Skype.   [The GSoC proposal is here.]
  • Cristina Turken from  the Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Transilvania University of Brasov, Romania with her submission to create a GUI-based desktop application that allows software publishers to create a Outercurve CoApp package, (instead of using the command line tools). [The GSoC proposal is here.]

While we were very excited to participate in the Google Summer of Code, we had a problem.  These were not the only two excellent proposals we received and discussed with students.  The Outercurve Mayhem and Outercurve CoApp projects each wanted to support at least one more student proposal.  The project leaders (finding budget at Microsoft) and Outercurve jointly funded two more student proposals.  The two "Outercurve" Summer of Code projects accepted were:

  • Henrik Juvonen in his second year of computer science at the University of Turku in Finland wanted to build CoApp for Visual Studio so that CoApp library packages (native C/C++ and .NET) can be discovered, installed, and updated without ever having to leave the development environment.
  • Anirudh Ranganath in his final undergrad year in engineering studying computer science in India wanted to add a number of events and responses to Mayhem for monitoring charging states, noting USB device connect/disconnect, and an Alarm event, as well as putting the computer in different power modes (sleep, hibernation, shut down) and setting the volume level.

All of the projects are wrapping up, final mentor reports are in, and final checks are being cut.  Over the next few weeks we will showcase the students work.  I’d like to thank Google for the opportunity to participate, the project leaders for finding the funds to co-sponsor additional student projects, and our mentors (Garrett Serack, Eli White, Eric Schultz, Elizabeth Smith).  I would especially like to thank our students for all their hard work.  I hope they all found the summer worthwhile and will continue to participate with their Outercurve Foundation projects of choice.
