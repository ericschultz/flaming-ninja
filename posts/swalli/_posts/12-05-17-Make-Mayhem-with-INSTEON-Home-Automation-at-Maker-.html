--- title: "Make Mayhem with INSTEON Home Automation at Maker Faire" alias: /Blogs/EntryId/54/Make-Mayhem-with-INSTEON-Home-Automation-at-Maker-Faire ---

Mayhem, one of Outercurve’s newest projects, is rapidly gaining traction in the developer community.  Mayhem is an open source scripting engine for non-programmers which connects events with actions.  An outstanding example of Mayhem in action will be showcased at this weekend’s Bay Area Maker Faire, scheduled for May 19-20 in San Mateo.  In addition to the Mayhem team, Dave Templin and colleagues from SmartLabs will be showing a Mayhem-amped version of their INSTEON home automation technology. (SmartLabs has released the Mayhem software modules as open source as well.)

INSTEON adds remote control and automation to lighting, appliance and home control applications of all types. It uses Mayhem’s simple event-reaction scripting system as an interface to INSTEON components, enabling home-automation fans to interconnect services and devices to stir things up at home. Here are a few of the possibilities:

  • Control your pets: point an INSTEON motion sensor at the couch. When it detects your cat/dog/pet of choice jumping up, your computer can yell in your stead.
  • Control your kids: see #1.
  • Turn the lights on at home – from your office - using the Windows Phone module in conjunction with the INSTEON module.
  • Left the coffeemaker or iron on when you went to work? No problem; turn them off.
  • Act like a spy: set secret passwords to unlock the front door with voice commands.

As an incentive for Mayhem fans, SmartLabs will be raffling INSTEON starter kits at the event.  

And don’t forget the Make Your Own Mayhem contest – with Mayhem, you’ll be able to dream up lots of your own home automation tasks – anything which can be coded with a simple action-reaction script.

So if you’re in San Mateo this weekend, stop by Maker Faire and visit the Mayhem folks in the Microsoft booth.
