--- title: "Speakers at the Outercurve Open Source Software Conference, 2013" alias: /Blogs/EntryId/92/Speakers-at-the-Outercurve-Open-Source-Software-Conference-2013 ---

We’re only a few weeks away from the first Outercurve Open Source Software Conference (May 8-9, 2013 in Bellevue, WA).  I’m really excited about our speaker list!  The conference will get underway with Jono Bacon’s keynote on "Taking Your Community Across the Chasm".  Jono runs the Ubuntu community, is the author of “The Art of Community”, and organizes the the Community Leadership Summit each year before O’Reilly’s OSCON in July.  Donnie Berkholz will be presenting how “A**holes are Ruining your Community”.  Donnie has been a leader in the Gentoo community for sometime, as well as being one of the core analysts at Redmonk.  We also have Ross Gardler talking about open source governance models. Ross now works for Microsoft Open Technologies, but Ross has years of experience with the Apache Software Foundation and spent a long time at OSS Watch.  He’s also an Outercurve mentor.  

Two speakers will provide context for the creation of the Outercurve Foundation.  Microsoft corporate vice-president, Scott Guthrie, will talk about how his division works with Outercurve, its importance, and lead a Q&A.  Sam Ramji, Outercurve board president, and the person that set the Outercurve Foundation in motion will also provide a view of Outercurve and where it’s going.  

We also have a set of Outercurve project leaders sharing what they’ve learned in building some of Outercurve’s larger communities with Bertrand Le Roy talking about the Orchard Project, and Phil Haack talking about the NuGet community.  We will be putting Garrett Serack (CoApp), Rob Mensching (WiX), and Jeff Handley (NuGet) up on stage as well for Outercurve Fight Club to demonstrate what a little creative disagreement looks like in a battle of the Outercurve package installation projects.  Lastly, I’ll be introducing a framework for how to think about organizing an open source software community for growth and success.  

After the presentations, if you want to roll up your sleeves and code, Garrett Serack will be hosting a 2-day hackathon on Friday and Saturday over on the Microsoft campus.   We are bringing together folks from different Outercurve open source projects like NuGet, CoApp, and Orchard along with engineers from some Microsoft product groups.   Prizes, drinks, and a whole lot of code!   You can register for this event via the OuterConf registration process.

So there’s lots to see.  There’s lots to learn.  (And we’re still working on a last couple of speakers.)  Please come out and join us for the first Outercurve Open Source Software Conference.
