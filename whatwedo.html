Summary

The mission of the Outercurve Foundation is to enable the exchange of code and understanding among software companies and open source communities. We do this by providing a skilled team of open source advocates who provide what we call "foundation in a box" services. In practice, this means we provide the basic business building blocks open source projects need as they grow, from software IP management and project development governance, to enable and encourage organizations to develop software collaboratively in open source communities for faster results. Our experience working with Enterprise software companies has allowed us to develop demonstrated neutrality which allows us to support projects regardless of their license, technology base, or development process, and has helped us develop a cost effective alternative to starting your own non-profit. We are committed to fostering industry growth by extending the benefits of neutrality, a liability firewall for contributors, and sharing our excellence at packaging open source IP.

Services for Projects

Outercurve Foundation provides staffing administration and operational expertise to deliver the functions of a .org for its Projects.  The following services are available to all Outercurve Projects.  

Project Guidance and Support

Administration

Outercurve Foundation handles all administrative burdens on behalf of its Projects, including: processing and tracking individual contributor agreements, compiling monthly Project stats, maintaining Project page on www.outercurve.org and more.

IP and Legal Affairs

We organize and manage IP assignments and contribution agreements, and provide regular reports to project leaders.  We also provide committer indemnification and legal services to projects through our outside counsel. 

Leadership Mentoring and Guidance

Our Mentorship program provides Project leaders with access to experienced developers with expertise in open source community dynamics. Mentors work with Project Leaders to help grow the Project community and develop technology to allow projects to become the best they can be.

Authenticode Code-Signing

Projects can have their binaries and installation packages Authenticode signed with Outercurve's certificate from Verisign.

Meeting/Event Support

Outercurve Foundation can provide logistical support for Project meetings/events, including: financial responsibility and liability, setting up registration sites, registration reports and name badges, ordering collateral and more. 

In the event a Project needs to negotiate and execute a contract with a meeting venue or service provider, Outercurve can assist Projects by helping to negotiate contracts, and can sign the contract on behalf of the Project.

PR/Marketing Support

Outercurve Foundation can provide PR and marketing guidance and support. On the PR front, we work with project leaders to develop press strategies around major project milestones.  We also support project marketing efforts by providing guidance on website branding, collateral design and production, and more. 

Project Donations

Outercurve Foundation can process earmarked donations on behalf of the individual Projects.  Project donations are kept in separate accounts by Outercurve. This prevents Project leaders from having to commingle Project funds with personal accounts or having to establish Project specific accounts.

As a tax-exempt organization [501 (c)(6)] , there are some limits on what Projects can do with their assets, but those limits are the same as if the Project was an independent non-profit entity.  Project leaders and Outercurve staff work together to establish how the Project funds are spent.  Outercurve spends the funds on the Project's behalf on expenses that constitute appropriate activity under the Outercurve mission.  Following are some examples of how earmarked Project donations are spent:

  • funding travel expenses for project developers to attend relevant conferences.
  • domain name fees, bandwidth costs, and computer equipment purchases.
  • purchasing media for distribution of project software at conferences and events.
  • paying key developers on a contractual basis to improve the project's software and its documentation.
  • sponsoring and organizing conferences for the project.

In addition to processing project donations, Outercurve can provide tools and advice to Projects on how to raise funds.

Discounts and Special Offers

Outercurve Project Contributors can receive complimentary licenses for software development tools from JetBrains. 

The free licenses include JetBrains’ award-winning integrated development environments (IDEs), and the company’s .NET and mobile development tools. Outercurve project contributors may also use YouTrack, an agile project management tool, and TeamCity, a continuous integration and build management server. Those tools are available through CodeBetter, which is a collaboration of Devlicio.us, IdeaVine and JetBrains.

To learn more, contact Outercurve Developer Advocate.

MSDN Subscriptions

Project Contributors have access to complementary MSDN subscriptions giving them access to copies of Visual Studio, Windows, Office and other Microsoft products for development purposes.
